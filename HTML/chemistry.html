CHEMICAL SOLUTIONS

Chemistry is the science that studies the property of matter in order to create and discover new phenomena since chemistry is everywhere such as medicine, industry, environment, among others.

WHAT IT IS?

A solution is a mixture of two or more substances to form a homogeneous mixture that is when they lose their individual characteristics. It comes as solid, liquid and gas.

CHARACTERISTICS

  • Its components cannot be divided by simple physical methods such as centrifugation, decantation, filtration, among others.
  • Its components can only be separated by distillation, crystallization, chromatography.
  • The components of a solution are solute and solvent.

Solute:

Which is dissolved by the solvent

Dissolvent:

The one that dissolves the solute

For example, the sugar is dissolved by water. In this case, the sugar is the solute and the dissolvent is the water.

There are three types of concentration: Unsaturated, saturated and over-saturated

  • Unsaturated: when there is less solute than solvent.
  • Saturated: when there is a balance between solute and solvent.
  • Over-saturated: when there is more solute than solvent.

FORMULA

  • C=m/V

  • C=Concentration

  • m= Mass of dissolved solute

  • V= Total volume of solution
